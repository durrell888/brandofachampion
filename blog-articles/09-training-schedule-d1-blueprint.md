## The Three Seasons of Training

Football training isn't one-size-fits-all year-round. Your body needs different things at different times.

### Offseason (January - May): BUILD

This is where you become a different athlete. The offseason is for gaining strength, adding muscle, improving speed, and addressing weaknesses.

**Weekly Schedule:**

**Monday — Lower Body Strength**
- Squats: 4 sets x 5 reps (heavy)
- Romanian Deadlifts: 3 sets x 8 reps
- Bulgarian Split Squats: 3 sets x 10 each leg
- Leg Press: 3 sets x 12 reps
- Calf Raises: 4 sets x 15 reps
- Core circuit: 3 rounds (planks, Russian twists, leg raises)

**Tuesday — Upper Body Strength + Speed**
- Bench Press: 4 sets x 5 reps (heavy)
- Barbell Rows: 4 sets x 8 reps
- Overhead Press: 3 sets x 8 reps
- Pull-ups: 3 sets to failure
- Speed work: 6x40 yard sprints (full recovery between reps)

**Wednesday — Active Recovery**
- Light jogging or swimming (20 minutes)
- Full body stretching (20 minutes)
- Foam rolling
- Film study (watch college film at your position — 30 minutes)

**Thursday — Lower Body Power**
- Power Cleans: 4 sets x 3 reps
- Box Jumps: 4 sets x 5 reps
- Trap Bar Deadlifts: 4 sets x 5 reps
- Walking Lunges: 3 sets x 12 each leg
- Sled pushes: 4 x 20 yards
- Agility ladder work: 10 minutes

**Friday — Upper Body Power + Conditioning**
- Incline Bench: 4 sets x 6 reps
- Weighted Dips: 3 sets x 8 reps
- Face Pulls: 3 sets x 15 reps
- Dumbbell Rows: 3 sets x 10 each arm
- Conditioning: 10 x 100 yard sprints (30 seconds rest)

**Saturday — Position Work + Skills**
- Position-specific drills (1 hour)
- Route running, footwork, technique work
- Competitive 1-on-1 situations
- Film review of your own performance

**Sunday — Complete Rest**
- Sleep. Eat. Recover. Your body grows when you rest, not when you train.

### Pre-Season (June - August): SHARPEN

Transition from building strength to building football-specific conditioning.

**Key changes:**
- Reduce max-effort lifting to 3 days per week
- Increase conditioning volume — your body needs to handle the heat and the grind of two-a-days
- Add more position-specific work
- Focus on football movements: backpedaling, lateral shuffles, explosive starts
- Start practicing game-speed situations

**Weekly emphasis:**
- Monday/Thursday: Strength maintenance (lower intensity, higher speed)
- Tuesday/Friday: Conditioning + position skills
- Wednesday: Speed and agility focused
- Saturday: Competitive drills and scrimmage situations
- Sunday: Rest

**Conditioning benchmark:** By the end of pre-season, you should be able to run sixteen 110-yard sprints in under 18 seconds each with 30 seconds rest between reps. That's the standard many college programs use for conditioning tests.

### In-Season (August - November/December): MAINTAIN

The biggest mistake high school athletes make during the season: they stop training.

Your body doesn't maintain itself. If you only practice and play games, you will lose strength and explosiveness as the season goes on. The teams that finish strong are the ones that maintain their training.

**In-season training (2-3 days per week):**

**Monday (after Saturday game):**
- Light recovery workout
- Upper body: moderate weight, higher reps (3 sets x 10-12)
- Core work
- Stretching and mobility

**Wednesday:**
- Lower body: moderate weight, explosive movements
- Squats: 3 sets x 5 (75-80% of max)
- Power Cleans: 3 sets x 3
- Box Jumps: 3 sets x 5
- Keep it under 45 minutes — don't fatigue yourself before game week

**Do NOT lift heavy on Thursday or Friday.** Your legs need to be fresh for game day.

## Nutrition: The Part Everyone Ignores

You can follow the perfect training program and still not improve if your nutrition is garbage.

**The basics:**
- **Protein:** 1 gram per pound of bodyweight, daily. Chicken, beef, fish, eggs, Greek yogurt, protein shakes.
- **Carbs:** Your fuel. Rice, potatoes, oatmeal, pasta, fruit. Don't cut carbs — you're an athlete, not a model.
- **Water:** Minimum half your bodyweight in ounces per day. More during training and games.
- **Meals:** Eat 4-5 times per day. Three meals plus snacks. You can't build a D1 body on two meals a day.

**Pre-game meal (3-4 hours before):**
- Lean protein + complex carbs + low fat
- Example: grilled chicken, rice, steamed vegetables
- Avoid heavy, greasy, or new foods you haven't eaten before

**Post-workout:**
- Protein shake or meal within 30 minutes of training
- Protein + carbs to recover and rebuild

**What to avoid:**
- Fast food as a regular habit (occasional is fine)
- Sugary drinks — switch to water
- Skipping meals — your body can't build what it doesn't have fuel for
- Energy drinks before games — the crash will hurt you in the second half

## Sleep: The Secret Weapon

This isn't a wellness blog lecture. This is real: sleep is when your body recovers and grows.

**Minimum 8 hours per night.** Ideally 9 during heavy training periods.

The science is clear: athletes who sleep less than 7 hours per night have higher injury rates, slower reaction times, and worse decision-making. You're literally making yourself worse by staying up late.

Put the phone down at 10 PM. Set an alarm for 6 or 7 AM. Your body will thank you, and your performance will show it.

## The Mental Side

Training your body is half the battle. Training your mind is the other half.

**Weekly mental training habits:**
- **Film study:** Watch 30 minutes of college film at your position, twice per week. Study technique, not just highlights.
- **Visualization:** Before bed, spend 5 minutes visualizing yourself executing plays perfectly. This sounds soft. It works.
- **Goal setting:** Write down your goals for the week, the month, and the season. Review them every Sunday.
- **Accountability:** Find a training partner who pushes you. Iron sharpens iron.
