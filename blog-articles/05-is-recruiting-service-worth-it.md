## How Recruiting Services Work

At their core, recruiting services claim to help high school athletes gain exposure to college coaches. They typically offer some combination of:

- Recruiting profiles on their platform
- Film hosting and distribution
- Databases of college programs and coaches
- Direct contact with coaching staffs
- Camp and showcase opportunities
- Evaluation and rating of athletes

The idea is simple: most high school athletes don't have the connections or knowledge to navigate recruiting on their own. A service bridges that gap.

The question is whether the bridge they're building is real or made of cardboard.

## The Big Names: What You're Actually Getting

### NCSA (Next College Student Athlete)

NCSA is one of the largest recruiting services in the country. They offer profile building, coach matching, and recruiting guidance.

**The good:** Large database, organized platform, some athletes do find opportunities through them.

**The concern:** Packages can cost $500 to $3,000+. Many families report that after signing up, the "personalized" attention they were promised turns into generic emails and automated matching. NCSA works with hundreds of thousands of athletes — your son isn't getting individual attention at that scale.

**Bottom line:** NCSA can be a useful tool as part of a broader strategy, but it's not a magic bullet. And for what it costs, you should expect more than a profile page.

### Hudl

Hudl is primarily a film platform — and it's one of the best for hosting and sharing highlight videos. Many high schools use Hudl for game film, and coaches are familiar with the platform.

**The good:** Excellent film hosting, widely recognized by coaches, many features are free or low-cost through your high school.

**The concern:** Hudl is a tool, not a service. Having your film on Hudl doesn't mean anyone is watching it. You still need to actively send it to coaches.

**Bottom line:** Every athlete should have their film on Hudl. But don't confuse having a Hudl profile with being recruited. It's a hosting platform, not a recruiting service.

### 247Sports / Rivals / MaxPreps

These are media and ranking platforms. They cover recruiting, rank athletes, and provide exposure through their coverage.

**The good:** If you're ranked or featured by these platforms, it generates significant buzz. Coaches pay attention to their rankings.

**The concern:** Getting ranked requires you to already be on the radar. These platforms don't create opportunities for unknown athletes — they amplify athletes who are already being recruited.

**Bottom line:** Great for top-tier prospects. Not very useful for the 95% of athletes who aren't already getting D1 attention.
